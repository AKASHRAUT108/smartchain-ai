
SUPPLY CHAIN MANAGEMENT GUIDE
==============================

DEMAND FORECASTING BEST PRACTICES
-----------------------------------
Demand forecasting accuracy depends on historical data quality.
Use at least 2 years of historical data for seasonal products.
Incorporate external signals like weather, holidays, and economic indicators.
Safety stock formula: Safety Stock = Z-score × Standard Deviation × Lead Time.
Reorder point = Average Daily Usage × Lead Time + Safety Stock.
ABC analysis: A-items (top 20% revenue), B-items (middle), C-items (bottom 50%).

SUPPLIER RISK MANAGEMENT
-------------------------
Always maintain at least 2-3 alternative suppliers per critical component.
Supplier risk score = (delivery performance × 0.4) + (quality score × 0.4) + (financial stability × 0.2).
High risk suppliers: delivery rate below 85% or quality defect rate above 2%.
Conduct supplier audits every 6 months for critical suppliers.
Geopolitical risk zones: Southeast Asia, Eastern Europe, Middle East require buffer stock of 30 extra days.

INVENTORY OPTIMIZATION
-----------------------
Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) = sqrt(2 × Demand × Ordering Cost / Holding Cost).
Just-In-Time (JIT) reduces inventory costs but increases supply chain vulnerability.
Recommended safety stock levels: A-items = 7 days, B-items = 14 days, C-items = 30 days.
Dead stock threshold: items with zero movement for 90+ days should be reviewed.
Inventory turnover ratio below 4 indicates overstocking problems.

LOGISTICS AND SHIPPING
-----------------------
Standard shipping modes: Standard (5-7 days), Express (2-3 days), Same-Day (24 hours).
Late delivery rate above 10% requires immediate supplier review.
Port congestion typically adds 3-7 days to ocean freight timelines.
Air freight costs 4-6x more than ocean freight but reduces lead time by 70%.
Last-mile delivery accounts for 53% of total shipping costs.

DISRUPTION RESPONSE PROTOCOLS
-------------------------------
Level 1 disruption (minor): Switch to backup supplier within 48 hours.
Level 2 disruption (moderate): Activate safety stock, notify customers of potential delays.
Level 3 disruption (severe): Emergency procurement, airfreight critical items, executive escalation.
Natural disaster protocol: Pre-position inventory in unaffected regions before storm season.
Strike protocol: Increase inventory by 25% in affected regions 2 weeks before anticipated strike.

KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS
---------------------------
Perfect Order Rate target: above 95%.
Inventory Accuracy target: above 99%.
Supplier On-Time Delivery target: above 92%.
Order Cycle Time: time from order placement to delivery.
Cash-to-Cash Cycle Time: days between paying suppliers and receiving customer payment.
Freight Cost per Unit: total freight cost divided by units shipped.

PORT STRIKE RESPONSE
---------------------
Immediate actions within 24 hours:

--------------------------
Earthquake or Typhoon protocol:
1. Immediately assess supplier facilities in affected region
2. Contact all tier-1 suppliers within 6 hours
3. Identify tier-2 and tier-3 backup suppliers
4. Calculate weeks of inventory coverage for all affected SKUs
5. Place emergency orders for items below 2 weeks coverage

Flood protocol:
1. Check warehouse locations for flood risk
2. Move inventory to higher ground facilities
3. Prioritize waterproof packaging for vulnerable goods
